Economics

Marks = diagram accuracy + clear explanation + evaluation (judgement).

Papers (SL vs HL)

  • SL: Paper 1 + Paper 2
  • HL: Paper 1 + Paper 2 + Paper 3 (3 papers)
  • Paper 1: essay response
  • Paper 2: data response + diagrams
  • Paper 3 (HL): quantitative + policy/application

What examiners want

  • Correct definitions (tight, not long)
  • Correct diagrams (labels, shifts, equilibrium changes)
  • Chain of reasoning (because → therefore → impact)
  • Evaluation (depends on context + judgement)

DEED structure (use this for 10-mark)

DEED

  • Define key terms
  • Explain the theory clearly
  • Example (real-world, relevant)
  • Diagram (proper labels + shifts)

For 15-mark add evaluation

  • Short-run vs long-run effects
  • Stakeholders (consumers/producers/government)
  • Assumptions / limitations
  • Conclusion with judgement (most likely outcome)

Diagram checklist (don’t lose free marks)

  • Axes labelled (Price/Quantity, Wage/Labour, etc.)
  • Curves labelled (D, S, MPC, MSB, AD, SRAS, LRAS)
  • Shift direction shown clearly (left/right)
  • New equilibrium labelled (P1, Q1)
  • Explain the shift using words (what changed and why)

Paper strategies

Paper 1 (essay)

  • Pick the question where you know the diagram instantly
  • Plan quickly: definitions + diagram + 2 chains of reasoning
  • Evaluation: at least 2 points + judgement

Paper 2 (data response)

  • Use the data given (quote it)
  • Don’t over-theorize: answer the question asked
  • Diagram must match the scenario in the data

Paper 3 (HL)

  • Show calculations cleanly + units where relevant
  • Interpret numbers (what the calculation means)
  • Link back to policy goals (inflation, unemployment, growth)

Common mark traps

Explanation traps

  • Describing the diagram without explaining why it shifts
  • No real example (or irrelevant example)
  • Using “it increases” without direction + mechanism

Evaluation traps

  • Listing pros/cons without judgement
  • No time frame (short vs long run)
  • Ignoring PED/PES when it matters

Study plan (Econ = diagram reps)

  • Weekly: 6 diagrams from memory (no notes), then check.
  • Weekly: 1 timed mini-essay (10–15 min) + improve it.
  • Paper 2 practice: answer with 2 data quotes minimum.
  • Build “evaluation phrases” you can reuse cleanly.
