Psychology

High marks come from: correct study choice + tight link to the question + evaluation.

Papers (SL vs HL)

  • SL: Paper 1 + Paper 2
  • HL: Paper 1 + Paper 2 + Paper 3
  • Paper 1: core approaches + ERQs/SAQs
  • Paper 2: option content essays
  • Paper 3 (HL): research methods / stimulus-based analysis

What examiners want

  • Accurate study details (aim, procedure, results)
  • Link studies back to the question (not just storytelling)
  • Evaluation that fits (not random limitations)
  • Clear structure (SAQ = tight; ERQ = planned)

Study bank template (use for every study)

Write your study bank like this

  • Aim: what they tested
  • Method: sample, design, procedure
  • Results: what they found
  • Link: how it answers the question
  • Eval: strengths/limitations (method-specific)

Best evaluation types

  • Validity (did it measure what it claims?)
  • Reliability (would it replicate?)
  • Sample bias / generalizability
  • Ethics (consent, deception, harm)

Paper strategies

SAQ (short answer)

  • Define the concept
  • Use ONE study (most of the time) and keep it tight
  • Link explicitly to the question
  • Don’t over-evaluate (unless asked)

ERQ (essay response)

  • Plan: intro + 2 studies + evaluation + conclusion
  • Always answer the command term (evaluate, discuss)
  • Link back to the question every paragraph

Paper 3 (HL)

  • Be fluent with: IV/DV, sampling, ethics, bias
  • Stimulus-based: quote the stimulus then analyze
  • Limitations must match the method used

Common mark traps

Study traps

  • Wrong details (sample size, procedure) = credibility loss
  • Using a study that doesn’t actually match the concept
  • Writing results without explaining meaning

Evaluation traps

  • Generic “small sample size” every time
  • No balance (only weaknesses, no strengths)
  • No conclusion/judgement

Study plan (Psych = study bank + timed writing)

  • Build 8–12 “core” studies you can use flexibly.
  • Weekly: write 1 timed SAQ + 1 timed ERQ paragraph plan.
  • After marking: rewrite one paragraph to markscheme quality.
  • Paper 3: practice research methods questions weekly (HL).
